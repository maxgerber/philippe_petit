Philippe Petit

Man on Wire

Phillipe Petit was a criminal, but his goals diverged from that of most criminals. His aim was emotion. He gave joy back to cities which could so often seem lifeless and unnurturing to the communities that inhabited them. Petit championed beauty and playfulness for its own sake; his acts symbolised freedom from the humdrum of the everyday. He was escapism dancing on the rooftops, a renegade, an embodiment of passion so consuming that it determines the direction of a life. This is why I find him inspiring.

a select few performances:

  • 1949 - Born in Nemours, Seine-et-Marne, France
  • 1971 - Put up a tightrope between the two towers of Notre Dame de Paris
  • 1974 - The most famous tightrope of Petit's career, between the two Twin Towers of New York. The last of his unauthorised performances
  • 1974 - After captivating the world, the state dropped all charges against Petit in exchange for another performance in Central Park
  • 1989 - Petit walks between the Palais de Challoit and the Eiffel Tower
